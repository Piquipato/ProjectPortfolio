## Projects in Software Development

<p align="center">
  <img src="figures/figure-1.png" alt="Projects in Software Development (Minecraft server deployment and card games utilities)" width="75%" />
</p>

The personal projects I have worked on over the years varied according to what I was interested in at the moment. 
For example, when I was younger, I loved to play Minecraft with my friends, and back at that time, everyone played on Minecraft servers. Partly motivated by the profits these servers were churning, and partly motivated by having fun with my friends, at the age of 16 I started learning programming as a self-taught learner and learned to manage and configure Minecraft third-party servers, using software like Spigot and Bukkit.

My most successful server deployment, which I mostly self-hosted at my old desktop computer back in my home, was during COVID-19. As we were locked down at home with nothing to do, most of my colleagues at university and at high school started playing Minecraft. I deployed a server that ran for over a year and that at its peak had 50 players connected at any given time.

I deployed servers both in vanilla and modded Minecraft, and to take control of versioning and deployment for my friends, I developed a simple custom launcher that I used to share game folders with my friends, with a very simple GUI and configured game settings.

Besides Minecraft, I started applying the knowledge I gathered in programming in smaller projects. I programmed utility scripts and small mobile phone apps to keep count in some popular betting card games played in Spain, like Mus and Pocha (check them out if you are curious, great games…).

## Simulations & Probability

<p align="center">
  <img src="figures/figure-2.png" alt="Simulations (Monte Carlo, Monopoly probabilities, and double pendulum)" width="75%" />
</p>

I also became interested in simulation and in calculating probability of events, doing scripts to perform Monte Carlo simulations and calculating probabilities of certain card combinations in these card games or of landing in certain streets in the famous game of Monopoly. Just before entering college, I became obsessed with simulating graphically a double pendulum with Processing in Java. I taught myself how to simulate this system with a Lagrangian formalism and the Java language.

## Electronics and Robotics

<p align="center">
  <img src="figures/figure-3.png" alt="Electronics and Robotics (Raspberry Pi / Arduino projects, FPV drones, and Lego Mindstorms)" width="75%" />
</p>

Outside of my interest in software, I also became interested in electronics and hardware. I became acquainted with the Raspberry Pi and the Arduino platforms at high school. With the Raspberry Pi, besides running home servers and Minecraft servers, I have built several handheld and desktop emulator systems for recreational use, using RetroPie OS. With Arduino, besides learning about electromagnetism and electronics during my degree, I prototyped a simple automatic water fountain for my cat, who used to wake me up at 3 am every morning to drink from the faucet, as he didn’t like still water from a bowl. Microcontroller knowledge has also allowed me to work with FPV drones that I now assemble at home.

I was also interested in robotics from an early age. At 14, we had a club at high school that I was one of the main members of that worked with Lego Mindstorms setups to build never seen before robots. At home, with the experience I was gaining in programming and prototyping at school, I managed to build a Humanoid robot capable of following set paths around my living room. I then built race cars with this setup as well.
